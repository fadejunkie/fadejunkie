fadejunkie Brand Guideline

1. Brand Identity & Tone
Mission: High-speed, high-quality web foundations for creators who don’t have time to waste.

Personality: Gritty Minimalist. It's raw and unpolished where it needs to be, but surgically precise in its execution.

Tone of Voice: Provocative, urgent, and high-octane.

Example: "Stop stalling. Your site should have been live yesterday. Let’s move."

2. Color Palette
The "Digital Noir" palette. High contrast with a "warning" accent.

Void Black: #000000 (Backgrounds, primary text)

Pure White: #FFFFFF (Secondary backgrounds, header text)

Anarchy Red: #E60000 (Accents, error states, "Buy" buttons, critical highlights)

Grit Gray: #1A1A1A (Card backgrounds, subtle dividers)

3. Logo Usage Rules
The skull is your "Mascot Mark." To keep it looking premium and not cluttered:

Clear Space: Maintain a "dead zone" around the skull equal to the width of the skull's jaw. No text or UI elements should enter this space.

Minimum Size: Do not scale the skull smaller than 32px for web; the spikes lose their sharpness.

Orientation: The skull must always face Left. It represents looking back at what was "normal" while the spikes point forward.

No Gradients: The logo remains strictly flat black or flat white. No 3D effects or shadows.

4. Typography
Headers: Inter Tight (Bold) or Archivo Black. All caps. Tight letter spacing.

Body Text: Courier Prime or IBM Plex Mono. This brings in the "Typewriter/Tech" feel.

Callouts: Use the Red accent for bold typewriter text to indicate high importance.

5. UI/UX: Grid & Components
The "Shattered" Grid System
Forget soft margins. fadejunkie uses a rigid, high-contrast grid.

The 12-Column Border: Use a 1px solid white border around the main container of the site.

Brutal Borders: Every section (Header, Main, Footer) is separated by a 1px or 2px solid white line. No soft shadows.

The "Dash" Divider: Use red dashed lines (----) to separate sections within a page, mimicking typewriter-style breaks.

Button Style Design
Buttons should feel like physical switches or punk patches.

Default State: Solid Black background, 2px Solid White border, White Typewriter text (All Caps). Square corners (0px border-radius).

Hover State: Background flips to Anarchy Red. Text stays White. The button "shifts" 2px up and 2px left with a black drop-shadow to create a "pressed" look.

The "Action" Button: For the most important CTA, use a White background with Black text and a Red border.

6. Iconography
Style: Solid, "Stencil" style icons.

Weight: Heavy. If using a "Home" icon, it should be a solid block, not an outline.

Execution: Keep icons small but impactful. Use them sparingly so the skull remains the dominant visual.

7. Imagery & Media
Treatment: All images should be Grayscale with "High Contrast" or "Threshold" filters applied.

Glitch: Use subtle horizontal "tear" effects on images to lean into the tech/hacker aesthetic.
