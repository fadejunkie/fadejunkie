## Your Direction, Confirmed

You chose **Warm Editorial** — and it was the right call. Everything we design from here starts with that warmth. Serif type that feels like a handwritten note, not a legal document. Photography that leads with people, not properties. A brand that reads as "real person who happens to be really good at this" before a single word of copy loads.

What you're about to see are three logo concepts built inside that direction. Each one interprets the warmth differently. None of them are final — they're starting points for a conversation.

---

## The Concepts

### Concept 1: The Imprint

Grounded. Established. The kind of mark that looks like it's been around for years — even on day one.

This concept leans into the editorial side of the direction. Think of the monogram or wordmark you'd see stamped on a cream-colored business card, or embossed on a folder handed to a client at closing. It's quiet confidence. The type does the work. No ornamentation competing for attention.

**Best suited for:** Yard signs, business cards, email signatures — anywhere the mark needs to stand alone at small sizes and still feel intentional.

---

### Concept 2: The Neighborhood

Warm. Rooted. Distinctly San Antonio.

This concept brings in a subtle visual element that ties the brand to place — not a generic "house" icon, but something that feels like it belongs to the neighborhoods Sydney actually works in. The warmth lives in the details: a softer letterform, a small accent that rewards a second look. It tells the viewer "this person knows where she is."

**Best suited for:** The website header, social media profiles, and branded content where you want the mark to carry personality — not just identification.

---

### Concept 3: The Signature

Personal. Distinctive. Unmistakably Sydney.

This concept feels the most human. It's the one that could only belong to one agent. There's a gestural quality to it — a nod to handwriting or a custom letterform that carries Sydney's name with warmth, not formality. It says "I'm the person you're going to work with" before the first phone call.

**Best suited for:** Hero placement on the website, printed collateral where you want the brand to feel personal (welcome packets, thank-you cards), and anywhere the audience is meeting Sydney's brand for the first time.

---

## What Happens Next

Pick the concept that feels most like you — or tell us which parts of different concepts you're drawn to. "I like the warmth of Concept 3 but the structure of Concept 1" is a perfectly useful note. Be as specific or as instinctive as you want. We'll take your direction and refine it into one polished mark with color, type, and layout variations ready for every surface.
