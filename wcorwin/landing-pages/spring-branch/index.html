Where the Hill Country opens up

If you've ever driven out of New Braunfels on US-281 heading northwest and felt the road open up — the live oaks getting taller, the fences getting further apart, the sky getting bigger — you've felt what makes Spring Branch different.

Spring Branch is an unincorporated community in Comal County, roughly 20 miles north of San Antonio and 15 miles northwest of New Braunfels. There's no downtown, no traffic lights to speak of, no strip malls lining the main road. What there is: rolling Hill Country terrain, acreage that actually feels like acreage, some of the best public schools in the region, and a pace of life built around the land.

Location and access

Spring Branch sits between New Braunfels to the southeast and Canyon Lake to the north. New Braunfels is 15 to 20 minutes south on US-281. Canyon Lake is 10 to 15 minutes north. Downtown San Antonio is about 40 to 45 minutes south. JBSA Randolph is roughly 35 minutes south. Austin is approximately 60 minutes north.

Despite the rural feel, Spring Branch is well connected. US-281 is a fast corridor, and proximity to New Braunfels means H-E-B, medical facilities, and restaurants within a 15-minute drive.

Land, schools, and community

This is where you come when you want land. Spring Branch properties frequently sit on 1 to 10+ acres, with many offering Hill Country views, mature oak groves, and the kind of setback from the road that's hard to find closer to metro areas.

Comal ISD is one of the primary reasons to choose Spring Branch. Bill Brown Elementary is consistently rated among the top elementaries in Comal County. Spring Branch Middle School has strong academics. Smithson Valley High School is the flagship campus — known for both academic performance and athletics.

Spring Branch has its own identity. A volunteer fire department, local churches, a community center, and the kind of small-town awareness where people recognize each other at the feed store. Annual events and an active residents' network create connection without the infrastructure of a municipality.

What to know before buying in Spring Branch

  • Well water and septic

    Nearly all Spring Branch properties are on private well water and septic systems. Have the well inspected for flow rate and water quality. Have the septic system inspected for age, capacity, and compliance. Budget for ongoing maintenance: septic pumping every 3 to 5 years, occasional well pump replacement.

  • Ag exemptions and property taxes

    Comal County property taxes average approximately 1.8%. Many Spring Branch properties qualify for agricultural or wildlife management exemptions, which can dramatically reduce the taxable value of the land. If you're buying 10+ acres, an ag exemption is worth thousands per year. Ask about the status of any existing exemption before closing.

  • Flood zones

    Most Spring Branch properties sit above the flood plain, but properties near the Guadalupe River or in low-lying drainage areas should be checked. Always verify FEMA flood zone status before you're under contract.

  • Internet and road maintenance

    Spectrum and several fixed-wireless providers serve the area, but availability varies by address — verify internet service before you're under contract if you work remotely. Some roads are county-maintained; others are private. Know what you're buying into.
