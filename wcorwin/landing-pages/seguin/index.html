Where the math works

Thirty-five miles east of San Antonio, where the Guadalupe River bends through pecan groves and the median home price still starts with a two, Seguin has quietly drawn buyers who want more house for their money and a town that still feels like a town.

Most people find Seguin after pricing out of New Braunfels or getting worn down by San Antonio's sprawl. They come looking for value and find a real community with a river running through the middle of it. Population has passed 33,000, and the growth is steady enough that the town made national lists for pace of residential expansion.

Where Seguin sits

Seguin is the county seat of Guadalupe County, positioned along I-10 between San Antonio and Houston. Downtown San Antonio is about 35 minutes west. New Braunfels is 15 to 20 minutes north via TX-46. JBSA Randolph is roughly 25 minutes west — making Seguin a practical home base for military families. Austin is about an hour north.

The Seguin lifestyle

The Guadalupe River runs through the center of Seguin. Max Starcke Park stretches along the river with kayaking, fishing, an 18-hole golf course, sports fields, and walking trails. Lake Placid, a dammed section of the Guadalupe, creates a calm swimming and recreation area right in town.

Downtown Seguin has a historic courthouse square, locally owned restaurants, antique shops, and a growing food and beverage scene. For daily needs, Seguin has H-E-B, Walmart, Home Depot, and a regional hospital. You are not driving to San Antonio for groceries.

Seguin ISD serves the majority of the city with consistently strong performance ratings. Texas Lutheran University adds a collegiate presence. Continental Automotive, Caterpillar, and several manufacturing and logistics operations provide local employment.

What to know before buying in Seguin

  • Property taxes

    Guadalupe County property taxes average approximately 2.0 to 2.2%, slightly higher than Comal County at 1.8%. But the lower purchase prices in Seguin often mean your total annual tax bill is comparable to or lower than a higher-priced home in New Braunfels or San Antonio.

  • Flood zones

    The Guadalupe River and its tributaries create FEMA flood zones in parts of Seguin. Properties near Lake Placid, Starcke Park, and low-lying areas along the river should always be checked for flood zone status before you make an offer. Flood insurance adds $1,500 to $3,000+ per year where required.

  • Utilities

    Most Seguin properties within city limits are on municipal water and sewer — a difference from Canyon Lake and Spring Branch where well and septic are standard. Rural properties outside city limits may be on well and septic. Verify before you make an offer.

  • Growth and development

    Seguin is growing, and that means construction activity, new commercial development, and changing traffic patterns. If you're buying in a quieter area, ask about planned developments nearby. The city's comprehensive plan shows expansion along the I-10 corridor and north toward New Braunfels.
