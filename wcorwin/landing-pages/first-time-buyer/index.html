Why first-time buyers choose New Braunfels

A lot of first-time buyers in the San Antonio–Austin corridor are landing in New Braunfels right now. It's affordable compared to Austin, has more character than most San Antonio suburbs, and sits close enough to both metros that the commute stays reasonable.

Affordability that still exists

The median home price in New Braunfels is around $339,000 in early 2026, down from peaks in 2022–2023. Homes priced between $225,000 and $325,000 are available in growing subdivisions and established neighborhoods throughout the city. Compare that to Austin ($475,000+ median) or north San Antonio ($385,000+), and the math speaks for itself.

Schools that perform

Both Comal ISD and New Braunfels ISD have earned Gold Performance Acknowledgements from the Texas Education Agency. If you're starting a family or planning ahead, the school quality here removes a major variable from the decision.

Location between two metros

New Braunfels sits on I-35, about 30 miles northeast of downtown San Antonio and 50 miles southwest of Austin. If your job is in either city, you can live here and commute. If you work remote, you get the space without the price tag — and the Comal River on weekends.

Step by step — how to buy your first home in New Braunfels

  1. Check your financial readiness

    Before browsing listings, get clear on where you stand. Credit score: most conventional loans require 620+. FHA loans go as low as 580. DTI: lenders generally want total monthly debt payments below 43% of gross income. Savings: plan for 2–5% in closing costs. On a $300,000 home, that's $6,000–$15,000.

  2. Get pre-approved (not just pre-qualified)

    A pre-approval letter is based on verified income, credit, and assets — unlike a pre-qualification. Sellers in New Braunfels will not take your offer seriously without one. We work with local lenders who specialize in first-time buyer programs: FHA, USDA, VA, TSAHC down payment assistance, and Conventional 97.

  3. Define your must-haves

    Think about commute direction (SA vs. Austin), school zones, flood plain status, and HOA preferences. Our agents live here — we'll help you sort signal from noise. With homes sitting 74+ days on average in early 2026, you have time to be deliberate.

  4. Tour homes and make an offer

    When you find the right home, we move quickly. We pull recent comps, put together a competitive offer, and negotiate price, repairs, closing cost contributions, and timeline. All TREC-required disclosures come through us. In early 2026, over 70% of listings have had price reductions — you have real leverage.

  5. Inspection, appraisal, and due diligence

    Home inspection ($350–$500): non-negotiable. We recommend local inspectors who know Hill Country construction, foundation issues, and well/septic systems. Appraisal confirms the home's value for your lender. Title search confirms clear ownership and no liens.

  6. Close and move in

    Closing in Texas typically takes 30–45 days from accepted offer. You sign loan documents, pay remaining closing costs, and receive your keys. If you're working with Weichert Corwin, you also receive our buyer rebate program as a closing credit — ask us how it applies to your purchase.

Common mistakes first-time buyers make

  • Skipping the pre-approvalWithout a pre-approval letter, sellers in New Braunfels won't take your offer seriously. Get this done before you start touring homes.
  • Ignoring flood zonesParts of New Braunfels, particularly near the Guadalupe and Comal rivers, sit in FEMA flood zones. Flood insurance can add $1,500–$3,000+ per year. Always check FEMA maps before making an offer.
  • Maxing out your budgetBeing approved for $350,000 doesn't mean you should spend $350,000. Leave room for property taxes (Comal County averages around 1.8%), homeowners insurance, and maintenance.
  • Not using a local agentNational portals show listings, but they don't tell you that a subdivision has a history of foundation issues, or that a "quiet street" is about to get commercial development next door. Local knowledge is the edge.
  • Forgetting closing costsBudget 2–5% of the purchase price. On a $300,000 home, that's $6,000–$15,000. Our buyer rebate helps offset this, but plan ahead.
