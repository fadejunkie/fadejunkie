How VA home loans work

The VA home loan program provides eligible service members with mortgage terms that no conventional loan matches.

Who qualifies?

  • Veterans with an honorable discharge and minimum service requirements (90 days wartime, 181 days peacetime)
  • Active-duty service members after 90 continuous days of service
  • National Guard and Reserve members after 6 years of service or 90 days of active-duty mobilization
  • Surviving spouses of service members who died in the line of duty or from a service-connected disability

You need a Certificate of Eligibility (COE), which your lender can pull electronically in most cases. We work with VA-specialized lenders who handle this process daily.

Texas Veterans Land Board — an extra advantage

Texas runs a state-level home loan program specifically for veterans through the Texas Veterans Land Board (VLB), operated by the General Land Office. The VLB provides fixed-rate home loans up to $832,750 in 15, 20, 25, or 30-year terms. Veterans with a 30% or higher VA disability rating qualify for a discounted interest rate.

That means Texas veterans have two paths to homeownership with below-market terms. Our team can help you figure out which program, or which combination, puts the most money in your pocket.

The buying process for VA loan buyers

  1. Get your COE and pre-approval

    We connect you with a VA-specialized lender in the New Braunfels and San Antonio area. They pull your COE, review your financials, and issue a pre-approval letter — usually within 48 hours.

  2. Search for your home

    With pre-approval in hand, we start looking. Browse listings on our site, tell us your must-haves, and our agents schedule tours. We know which neighborhoods in New Braunfels, Canyon Lake, Spring Branch, and Seguin work best for military commuters.

  3. Make an offer and negotiate

    We handle the offer, negotiate on your behalf, and make sure VA-specific requirements are met — including the VA appraisal. Our experience with VA transactions means we know how to structure offers that sellers accept, even in competitive situations.

  4. Inspection, appraisal, and VA compliance

    VA loans require the home to meet Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs). We flag potential issues before you're under contract so there are no surprises. Our team has seen hundreds of VA appraisals and knows what passes and what doesn't.

  5. Close and collect your rebate

    At the closing table, your buyer rebate appears as a credit on your closing disclosure. You sign, you get your keys, and you keep more of what you earned.
