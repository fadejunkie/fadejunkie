## H1: Seguin, TX — affordable Hill Country living on the Guadalupe River

Thirty-five miles east of San Antonio, where the Guadalupe River bends through pecan groves and the median home price still starts with a two, Seguin has quietly drawn buyers who want more house for their money and a town that still feels like a town.

Most people find Seguin after pricing out of New Braunfels or getting worn down by San Antonio's sprawl. They come looking for value and find a real community with a river running through the middle of it. Population has passed 33,000, and the growth is steady enough that the town made national lists for pace of residential expansion.

## H2: Where Seguin sits

Seguin is the county seat of Guadalupe County, positioned along I-10 between San Antonio and Houston. Downtown San Antonio is about 35 minutes west. New Braunfels is 15 to 20 minutes north via TX-46 or I-35. JBSA Randolph is roughly 25 minutes west, which makes Seguin a practical home base for military families stationed at Randolph. Austin is about an hour north. Houston is two and a half hours east.

The I-10 corridor gives Seguin connectivity that most small Texas towns lack. For commuters working in east San Antonio, Schertz, or the Randolph area, the drive is short and the housing costs are considerably lower.

## H2: The Seguin lifestyle

### River town

The Guadalupe River runs through the center of Seguin, and the town's rhythm follows it. Max Starcke Park stretches along the river with kayaking, fishing, an 18-hole golf course, sports fields, and walking trails. Lake Placid, a dammed section of the Guadalupe, creates a calm swimming and recreation area right in town. People jog along it in the morning, fish it in the afternoon, and gather along the banks on weekends.

### Small-town character with real amenities

Downtown Seguin has a historic courthouse square, locally owned restaurants, antique shops, and a growing food and beverage scene. The Seguin Outdoor Learning Center, the Heritage Museum, and seasonal festivals like Pecan Fest give the town a cultural identity that goes beyond geography.

For daily needs, Seguin has H-E-B, Walmart, Home Depot, medical clinics, and a regional hospital. You are not driving to San Antonio for groceries.

### Schools

Seguin ISD serves the majority of the city, with campuses that have earned consistent performance ratings. The district has invested in facilities and programs, and the student-to-teacher ratios compare favorably with larger metro districts. Some areas on the north side of Seguin fall within Navarro ISD, which also carries strong community support.

### Local economy

Seguin's economy goes beyond residential growth. Continental Automotive, Caterpillar, and several manufacturing and logistics operations provide local employment. Texas Lutheran University adds a collegiate presence. The city's proximity to San Antonio's east-side industrial corridor makes it a practical home base for workers in manufacturing, logistics, and military support roles.

## H2: Seguin neighborhoods

### Nob Hill
One of the most affordable neighborhoods in the Seguin area. Homes run $250,000 to $350,000. A good fit for first-time buyers and families looking for a solid home in a quiet area.

### Cordova Crossing
A newer subdivision on the growing side of Seguin with modern construction and community amenities. Homes range from $280,000 to $400,000.

### The Village of Mill Creek
A family-oriented community with a golf course, pools, and walking trails. Homes range from $300,000 to $420,000, with a mix of styles from traditional to Hill Country contemporary.

### Lake Placid area
Properties near Lake Placid and the Guadalupe River offer waterfront or water-adjacent living at a fraction of Canyon Lake prices. Homes range from $275,000 to $500,000, with some riverfront properties above that.

### Historic downtown adjacent
Older homes near the courthouse square and historic district offer character, walkability, and restoration potential. Prices range from $175,000 to $325,000 depending on condition and lot size.

### Acreage and rural properties
Guadalupe County acreage surrounding Seguin is available for buyers who want space. Properties on 2 to 10+ acres with homes or build-ready sites range from $200,000 to $600,000 and up.

## H2: What to know before buying in Seguin

### Property taxes
Guadalupe County property taxes average approximately 2.0 to 2.2%, slightly higher than Comal County at 1.8%. But the lower purchase prices in Seguin often mean your total annual tax bill is comparable to or lower than a higher-priced home in New Braunfels or San Antonio.

### Flood zones
The Guadalupe River and its tributaries create FEMA flood zones in parts of Seguin. Properties near Lake Placid, Starcke Park, and low-lying areas along the river should always be checked for flood zone status. Flood insurance, where required, adds $1,500 to $3,000+ per year.

### Utilities
Most Seguin properties within the city limits are on municipal water and sewer, which is a difference from Canyon Lake and Spring Branch where well and septic are standard. Rural properties outside city limits may be on well and septic.

### Growth and development
Seguin is growing, and that means construction activity, new commercial development, and changing traffic patterns. If you are buying in a quieter area, ask about planned developments nearby. The city's comprehensive plan shows residential and commercial expansion along the I-10 corridor and north toward New Braunfels.
