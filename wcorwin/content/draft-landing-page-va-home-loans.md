## H2: How VA Home Loans Work

The VA home loan program, administered by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, provides eligible service members with mortgage benefits that no conventional loan program can match:

### Key VA Loan Benefits

| Feature | VA Loan | Conventional Loan |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| Down payment | **$0** | 3–20% |
| Private mortgage insurance | **None** | Required below 20% down |
| Interest rates | **Typically lower** | Market rate |
| Closing costs | **Limited by VA** | Unrestricted |
| Funding fee | One-time (can be rolled in) | N/A |
| Prepayment penalty | **None** | Varies |

### Who Qualifies?

- **Veterans** with an honorable discharge and minimum service requirements (90 days wartime / 181 days peacetime)
- **Active-duty service members** after 90 continuous days of service
- **National Guard and Reserve members** after 6 years of service (or 90 days of active-duty mobilization)
- **Surviving spouses** of service members who died in the line of duty or from a service-connected disability

You'll need a **Certificate of Eligibility (COE)**, which your lender can pull electronically in most cases. We work with VA-specialized lenders who handle this process daily.

## H2: Texas Veterans Land Board (VLB) — An Extra Advantage

Texas offers something most states don't: a state-level home loan program specifically for veterans.

The **Texas Veterans Land Board (VLB)**, operated by the General Land Office, provides:

- Fixed-rate home loans up to **$832,750**
- Available in 15, 20, 25, or 30-year terms
- Veterans with a **30%+ VA disability rating** qualify for a discounted interest rate
- Can be used alongside or instead of a federal VA loan

This means Texas veterans have two paths to homeownership with below-market terms. Our team can help you determine which program — or combination — puts the most money in your pocket.

## H2: Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I use my VA loan benefit more than once?
Yes. VA loan benefits are reusable. Once you've paid off or sold a VA-financed home, your entitlement is restored and you can use it again.

### Is there a maximum loan amount for VA loans in Texas?
For veterans with full entitlement, there is no VA loan limit as of 2020. You can borrow above the conforming limit, though lenders may have their own caps.

### Do VA loans take longer to close?
Not necessarily. With an experienced lender and a real estate team that knows the VA process, closings typically take 30–45 days — the same as conventional transactions.

### What if I'm PCSing to San Antonio and want to buy in New Braunfels?
We work with relocating military families regularly. We can start the search remotely, schedule virtual tours, and have everything lined up before your report date. New Braunfels is a popular choice for JBSA-assigned personnel who want more space and lower costs than living inside the SA loop.

### Does the seller have to pay anything extra for a VA loan?
The VA limits certain closing costs that can be charged to the buyer, but sellers are not required to pay them. In practice, we negotiate seller concessions as part of the offer strategy.
