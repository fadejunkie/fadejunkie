## H1: Spring Branch, TX — Hill Country living with room to breathe

If you've ever driven out of New Braunfels on US-281 heading northwest and felt the road open up, the live oaks getting taller, the fences getting further apart, the sky getting bigger, you've felt what makes Spring Branch different.

Spring Branch is an unincorporated community in Comal County, roughly 20 miles north of San Antonio and 15 miles northwest of New Braunfels. It's not a city. There's no downtown, no traffic lights to speak of, no strip malls lining the main road. What there is: rolling Hill Country terrain, acreage that actually feels like acreage, some of the best public schools in the region, and a pace of life built around the land rather than the commute.

For buyers looking for space and quiet within reach of the water and the Hill Country without the density of New Braunfels or the sprawl of San Antonio, Spring Branch is worth the drive.

---

## H2: Location and access

Spring Branch sits at the intersection of US-281 and FM 311, positioned between New Braunfels to the southeast and Canyon Lake to the north. New Braunfels is 15 to 20 minutes south on US-281. Canyon Lake is 10 to 15 minutes north. Downtown San Antonio is about 40 to 45 minutes south via US-281 or I-35. JBSA Randolph is roughly 35 minutes south. Austin is approximately 60 minutes north via US-281 and TX-46.

Despite the rural feel, Spring Branch is well connected. US-281 is a fast, well-maintained corridor, and the proximity to New Braunfels means access to H-E-B, medical facilities, and restaurants within a 15-minute drive.

## H2: What living in Spring Branch is like

### Land and privacy

This is where you come when you want land. Spring Branch properties frequently sit on 1 to 10+ acres, with many offering Hill Country views, mature oak groves, and the kind of setback from the road that's hard to find closer to the metro areas. If you've been priced out of Austin acreage or can't find space in San Antonio, Spring Branch should be on your list.

### Schools

Spring Branch is served by Comal ISD, one of the highest-rated districts in the San Antonio metro. Bill Brown Elementary is consistently rated among the top elementaries in Comal County. Spring Branch Middle School has strong academics and community involvement. Smithson Valley High School is the flagship campus for the area, known for both academic performance and athletics.

For families with school-age children, Comal ISD is one of the primary reasons to choose Spring Branch over comparable rural areas in Blanco or Kendall County.

### Outdoor access

Canyon Lake is a 10 to 15 minute drive north. The Guadalupe River is accessible from multiple points in the area. The Hill Country itself is the recreation: hiking, cycling on ranch roads, horseback riding, hunting, and simply being on a piece of land where the nearest neighbor is a quarter-mile away.

### Community

Spring Branch has its own identity. A volunteer fire department, local churches, a community center, and the kind of small-town awareness where people recognize each other at the feed store. Annual events and an active residents' network create connection without the infrastructure of a municipality.

## H2: Spring Branch neighborhoods and communities

### Mystic Shores
A gated, master-planned community straddling the Spring Branch and Canyon Lake areas. Community amenities include a pool, boat ramp, and community center. Lots range from 0.25 to 2+ acres. Home prices run $350,000 to $600,000 and up.

### River Crossing
Located along the Guadalupe River between Spring Branch and Canyon Lake. River access, community pool, and walking trails. A family-friendly community with a mix of home sizes. Prices run $300,000 to $550,000.

### Comal Hills
An established area on the south side of Spring Branch, closer to New Braunfels. Mostly homes on 1 to 5 acre lots with a rural character. Prices range from $325,000 to $500,000 depending on acreage and improvements.

### Serenity Oaks
A newer community offering Hill Country-style homes on generous lots. Gated entry, architectural guidelines, and a quiet setting. Homes typically range from $375,000 to $550,000.

### Unincorporated acreage
A significant portion of Spring Branch real estate is raw land or homes on 5 to 20+ acre tracts. This is where you find custom-built homes, small ranches, and properties with wildlife management exemptions. Prices vary widely: $250,000 to $1,000,000 and up depending on acreage, improvements, and views.

## H2: What to know before buying in Spring Branch

### Well water and septic

Nearly all Spring Branch properties are on private well water and septic systems. This is standard for the area, but it requires attention during due diligence. Have the well inspected for flow rate and water quality. Have the septic system inspected for age, capacity, and compliance. Budget for ongoing maintenance: septic pumping every 3 to 5 years, occasional well pump replacement. Some areas have access to water supply cooperatives, which is worth asking about during your search.

### Property taxes and ag exemptions

Comal County property taxes average approximately 1.8%. Many Spring Branch properties qualify for agricultural or wildlife management exemptions, which can dramatically reduce the taxable value of the land. If you're buying 10+ acres, an ag exemption is worth thousands per year. Ask about the status of any existing exemption before closing.

### Flood zones

Most Spring Branch properties sit above the flood plain, but properties near the Guadalupe River or in low-lying drainage areas should be checked. Always verify FEMA flood zone status before you're under contract.

### Internet and utilities

Rural does not mean disconnected. Spectrum and several fixed-wireless providers serve the Spring Branch area. However, availability varies by address. Verify internet service before you're under contract, especially if you work remotely.

### Road maintenance

Some Spring Branch roads are county-maintained. Others are private. Know what you're buying into: private road maintenance can add ongoing costs and requires coordination with neighbors.

## H2: Frequently asked questions

### Is Spring Branch TX near Houston?
No. This is Spring Branch in Comal County, in the Texas Hill Country between New Braunfels and Canyon Lake. There is a separate Spring Branch neighborhood in Houston, but they are entirely different places.

### What school district serves Spring Branch?
Comal ISD, including Smithson Valley High School, Spring Branch Middle School, and Bill Brown Elementary. One of the top-rated districts in the San Antonio metro area.

### How far is Spring Branch from San Antonio?
Approximately 40 to 45 minutes to downtown San Antonio via US-281. JBSA Randolph is about 35 minutes south.

### Are there affordable homes in Spring Branch?
Homes in the $300,000 to $400,000 range exist in communities like River Crossing and some established areas. Raw land with a modular or manufactured home can bring the entry point lower. But the median is higher because most buyers are purchasing acreage.
